# Cooking Reference

## Safe Internal Temperatures (USDA)

| Food | Safe temperature | Rest time |
|------|-----------------|-----------|
| Beef, pork, lamb, veal (whole cuts) | 145°F / 63°C | 3 minutes |
| Ground beef / pork | 160°F / 71°C | None |
| Poultry (all, including ground) | 165°F / 74°C | None |
| Fish and shellfish | 145°F / 63°C | None |
| Eggs (whole) | 160°F / 71°C | None |
| Leftovers and casseroles | 165°F / 74°C | None |

**Danger zone:** 40–140°F (4–60°C). Do not leave perishable food in this range for more than 2 hours, or 1 hour if the ambient temperature is above 90°F (32°C).

## Core Techniques

**Sauté:** High heat, small amount of fat, small or thin pieces, frequent movement. Goal is browning and quick cooking. Pat food dry before sautéing — moisture causes steaming instead of searing.

**Sear:** Very high heat, minimal fat, and patience — do not move the food until it releases naturally from the pan. Develops the Maillard crust. Dry the surface of the protein completely before it goes in.

**Braise:** Brown the item first (optional but adds flavour). Add liquid to roughly half the height of the ingredient. Cover and cook at a gentle heat for a long time. Best for tough cuts (chuck, shoulder, shank, short ribs). Collagen converts to gelatin, producing a silky, rich result.

**Roast:** Dry oven heat, uncovered. High heat (400°F / 200°C+) for crisp exteriors; lower heat (325°F / 165°C) for gentle, even cooking. Always let roasted meat rest 5–15 minutes before cutting so juices redistribute.

**Steam:** Cook over (not in) boiling water. Preserves colour, texture, and nutrients. Ideal for delicate fish and most vegetables. Use a rack, steamer basket, or improvise with a colander over a pot.

**Poach:** Gentle simmer at 160–180°F (71–82°C) — the surface barely shimmers, not a rolling boil. Used for eggs, fish, chicken breast. Season the poaching liquid generously; it becomes the base of your sauce.

**Reduction:** Simmer a liquid over medium heat to evaporate water and concentrate flavour. Stir occasionally. Watch heat carefully as liquid thins — reduce to thin sauce consistency, not caramel.

## Knife Skills

- **Pinch grip:** Thumb and forefinger pinch the blade just forward of the handle; the remaining three fingers curl on the handle. This gives control and reduces fatigue.
- **Claw grip on the food:** Curl fingertips under with knuckles forward, touching the blade flat as a guide. The blade cannot reach the fingertips.
- **Rock chop:** Keep the tip of the knife on the board and rock the heel down through the ingredient. Draw the heel backward for slices.
- A sharp knife is always safer than a dull one — it requires less force and is less likely to slip.

## Common Substitutions

| Missing ingredient | Substitute |
|-------------------|------------|
| Buttermilk (1 cup) | 1 cup whole milk + 1 tbsp lemon juice or white vinegar; rest 5 min |
| Baking powder (1 tsp) | ¼ tsp baking soda + ½ tsp cream of tartar |
| Cake flour (1 cup) | ¾ cup + 2 tbsp plain/all-purpose flour + 2 tbsp cornstarch, sifted |
| Brown sugar (1 cup) | 1 cup white sugar + 1 tbsp molasses |
| Sour cream (1 cup) | 1 cup full-fat plain yoghurt |
| Egg (1, in baking) | 3 tbsp aquafaba (chickpea liquid); or ¼ cup unsweetened applesauce; or 1 tbsp ground flaxseed + 3 tbsp water (rest 5 min) |
| Fresh herbs | Dried at one-third the quantity (1 tbsp fresh = 1 tsp dried) |
| Whole milk (1 cup) | ½ cup evaporated milk + ½ cup water |
| Wine (for cooking) | Equal amount of unsalted stock + 1 tsp white wine vinegar or lemon juice |
| Unsalted butter (1 cup) | ¾ cup + 2 tbsp neutral oil; or same volume of ghee |

## Key Ratios

| Application | Ratio |
|-------------|-------|
| Pasta water (salt) | 1 tablespoon per 4 quarts (4 litres) — the water should taste pleasantly salty |
| Brine (basic wet brine) | 1 tablespoon salt per 1 cup water |
| Vinaigrette | 3 parts oil : 1 part acid (vinegar or citrus juice) |
| Pie / shortcrust dough | 3 parts flour : 2 parts fat : 1 part ice water |
| Pancakes | 1 cup flour : 1 cup liquid : 1 egg : 1 tbsp fat : 1 tsp baking powder |
| Rice (stovetop, absorption) | 1 part dry rice : 2 parts water |
| Polenta / grits | 1 part grain : 4 parts liquid |
| Simple syrup | 1 part sugar : 1 part water (simmer until dissolved, then cool) |
| Roux (thickening) | Equal weights butter and flour by weight |

## Flavour Building

**The sequence:** fat → aromatics (onion, garlic, ginger, shallot) → dry spices → liquid → salt → acid → finish.

**Season in layers,** not only at the end. Each stage is an opportunity to build flavour.

**Acid brightens everything.** A squeeze of lemon, a splash of vinegar, or a spoonful of yoghurt at the end lifts flat or heavy dishes. Add acid last — heat diminishes it.

**Salt is not merely "salty."** It suppresses bitterness and amplifies other flavours. Add it, wait 30 seconds, then taste. Always taste before seasoning further.

**Fat carries flavour** and provides mouthfeel. Finishing a sauce with a knob of cold butter (monter au beurre) enriches both texture and taste.

**Umami depth:** Stir in parmesan rind while simmering a sauce; add a small amount of fish sauce, soy sauce, miso, or anchovy (which dissolves completely in hot oil) for savoury depth that has no identifiable flavour of its own.

**Spice pairings by cuisine:**
- *Italian:* basil, oregano, rosemary, thyme, bay leaf
- *French:* thyme, bay, tarragon, chervil, parsley
- *Indian:* cumin, coriander, turmeric, garam masala, cardamom, mustard seed
- *Mexican:* cumin, dried chilli, Mexican oregano, epazote, cilantro
- *Chinese:* five spice, star anise, Sichuan pepper, ginger, scallion
- *Middle Eastern:* cumin, coriander, cinnamon, sumac, za'atar, allspice

## Pantry Cooking (When Fresh Ingredients Are Low)

- Pasta + canned tomatoes + olive oil + garlic + chilli flake = arrabbiata
- Canned chickpeas + canned tomatoes + cumin + coriander + garam masala = chana masala
- Rice + canned black beans + cumin + smoked paprika + lime = rice and beans
- Dried red lentils + canned tomatoes + cumin + stock = lentil soup (30 min, no soaking required)
- Rolled oats + any nut butter + honey + dried fruit + nuts = overnight oats or no-bake energy bars
- Canned tuna + olive oil + capers + lemon + pasta = tuna pasta (10 min)

## Recipe Interpretation Tips

- *Bake at 350°F until done* usually means 30–45 min for a cake layer; insert a toothpick and it should come out clean.
- *Fold* means use a large spatula in a gentle J-motion to preserve air; never stir.
- *Al dente* means pasta still has the very slightest resistance at the centre — finish cooking in the sauce.
- *Deglaze* means add liquid to a hot pan and scrape up the browned bits (fond); this is the flavour.
- *Rest* after roasting means loosely tent with foil and wait — do not skip this step.
